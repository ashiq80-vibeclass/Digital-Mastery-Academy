Structured Path

Beginner to Professional — 3 Phases

Follow this structured path from complete beginner to professional-level skill. Each phase builds on the previous one.

Phase 1 — Beginner

  • What copywriting is and how it differs from content writing
  • The AIDA framework — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
  • Writing headlines and opening lines that hook readers
  • Understanding the target audience and their pain points

Phase 2 — Intermediate

  • Facebook and Google ad copy — short form and CTA structure
  • Sales page anatomy — headline, subheadline, benefits, testimonials, CTA
  • Handling objections in copy — price, trust and urgency
  • Email copy — subject lines, body and call-to-action

Phase 3 — Professional

  • Writing long-form sales letters from scratch
  • Before-and-after copy transformation exercises
  • Pricing copywriting services and building a portfolio
  • Creating a swipe file and developing your personal writing voice

Practical Task

Write 5 Facebook ad copy variations for one product

Practice writing short-form copy with different hooks and angles.
Step-by-Step Method
  1. Choose a real product or service you are familiar with.
  2. Write 5 different opening lines — curiosity, fear, benefit, question, bold statement.
  3. For each opening line, write 2 to 3 body sentences and one CTA.
  4. Read each variation aloud and rate them by which one feels most persuasive.
  5. Submit the best 2 variations as your final deliverable.
Deliverable5 complete Facebook ad copy variations with opening hook, body and CTA.
Pro TipThe strongest ad copy sounds like it was written for one specific person — not a crowd.
Practical Task

Write a complete sales page for a digital product

Learn to structure a full sales page from headline to purchase button.
Step-by-Step Method
  1. Write a main headline that states the biggest benefit or transformation.
  2. Write a subheadline that addresses the reader's main objection.
  3. List 5 to 7 benefits (not features) of the product.
  4. Write a testimonial placeholder section with 2 example formats.
  5. Write the pricing section with urgency or bonus offer language.
  6. Write the final CTA button text and a risk-reduction guarantee statement.
DeliverableA complete sales page structure in Google Docs — ready to paste into any landing page builder.
Pro TipBenefits answer 'what is in it for me?' — features answer 'what is it?' — always lead with benefits.
Practical Task

Rewrite weak copy to make it stronger

Practice the single most important skill in copywriting — editing and improving.
Step-by-Step Method
  1. Take any generic product description or social media bio and paste it into a doc.
  2. Identify all vague words — amazing, great, best, world-class — and replace them.
  3. Rewrite every passive sentence into active voice.
  4. Add one specific number, statistic or time frame to increase credibility.
  5. Compare the before and after versions and highlight the key differences.
DeliverableA before-and-after document showing the original and improved copy.
Pro TipSpecific always beats vague. '37% faster results' is stronger than 'noticeably better results'.
Teaching Flow

Recommended Teaching Sequence

  • Show a real high-converting sales page and break down every section.
  • Write a headline together as a class and compare 10 variations.
  • Give students a weak ad copy sample and ask them to improve it in 10 minutes.
  • Share examples from the Facebook Ad Library and explain what makes them work.
  • Review student sales page drafts and give specific line-by-line feedback.
Earning Direction

How to Monetize This Skill

  • Offer Facebook ad copywriting packages per ad set on Fiverr and Upwork.
  • Write landing page and sales page copy for online course creators and coaches.
  • Work as a freelance email copywriter — per campaign or monthly retainer.
  • Specialize in one niche such as health, finance or SaaS for higher rates.
